Physics

With an interest in the sciences and in the way the world works I chose to study Physics. Physics is the study of how the universe behaves on scales from galaxy clusters to the inside of atoms.

Ofcourse, in Physics I would learn about our world with courses like classical mechanics, special relativity, quantum mechanics, optics, astronomy, thermal physics, and electrodynamics, but I also had courses like math, practicals and research projects.

The behaviour of the universe can be described with math and so math is everywhere in the field of physics. In addition to knowledge of physics I have also developed mathematical skills in which critical thinking is important. To solve a problem in math, you consider what you know and how to apply that knowledge to get to a solution. In that regard, programming is not very different.

Programming in physics

During my study of Physics I first came into contact with programming. I have learned to use programming to apply math, to display data in meaningful ways, and to simulate physical phenomena.

Introduction in Python

The first time I had learned anything about programming was during the programming courses of my study of physics. I was taught the basics of Python like printing statements, mathematical operations, loops, and defining functions.

I would say the main purpose of learning programming in physics is to apply math, like mathematical operations, equation solving, or implementing mathematical functions, to analyse (experimental) data to find meaningful relations or patterns within that data and that was what I was taught in the programming courses.

Simulations

Since our universe follows rules that can be described by math it is also possible to simulate those rules via programming. For example, I have learned to simulate the trajectory of charged particles among static charges or the Electric potential field of different charge distributions.
